# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary users for the expert-side app are hometown-affiliated physicians practicing outside Mengcheng who review remote consultation requests on mobile devices between clinical duties. They need to scan patient context, accept or schedule consultations, review supporting materials, join image/video consultations, submit structured opinions, manage availability, and understand their service contribution without distraction.

## Product Purpose

The platform connects local doctors and patients in Qiyuan/Mengcheng with trusted external experts for remote diagnosis support, follow-up guidance, case review, and collaborative consultation. Success means doctors can move from pending work to a defensible clinical recommendation quickly, with clear records, stable layouts, and no ambiguity around status or next action.

## Brand Personality

Professional, warm, restrained. The interface should feel like a reliable medical work tool with a quiet hometown connection, not a campaign site or decorative showcase.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-page composition, oversized hero sections, exaggerated gradients, neon colors, large dark backgrounds, glassy card stacks, purple-heavy AI aesthetics, novelty interactions, and anything that slows a physician's high-frequency workflow.

## Design Principles

1. Clinical hierarchy first: patient identity, risk, chief complaint, attachments, timeline, and advice must be easy to scan.
2. Warmth through restraint: hometown identity appears as small honor and contribution cues, not decorative sentimentality.
3. State clarity over decoration: pending, scheduled, unavailable, urgent, completed, and follow-up states must be visually distinct and textually clear.
4. Stable mobile workbench: compact controls, predictable navigation, and fixed information regions should support repeated use on a phone.
5. Preserve workflow trust: visual upgrades must not alter business meaning, routing, permissions, APIs, fields, or state transitions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for body text and controls. Support long reading sessions with sufficient type contrast, predictable focus states, non-color-only status cues, stable tap targets, and layouts that avoid text overflow on narrow mobile screens.
